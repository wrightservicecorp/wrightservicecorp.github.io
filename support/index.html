Getting Started

Wright Service Corp. University (WSCU) is an online learning platform used to document, track, report, and deliver customized training that will help you learn skills and provide training essential to your role. It is recommended to use Google Chrome as your web browser for the best user experience. To get started with training on WSCU, navigate to your transcript to launch assigned training. You can also take search for elective courses on the home page.

Assigned training

If you have been assigned training, you will need to access your transcript to launch the course. To do so, go to the Learning tab on the blue navigation bar and select View Your Transcript. From there, you can see assigned, in progress, past due, and completed training. To launch a course, select the blue Launch button to the right of the title.

Search for training on the home page

After you log in, you will be redirected to the learner home page which will display a snapshot of your profile and list featured training available to you. You can search for courses from the top search bar by typing in key words. If you would like to see everything available to you, conduct an empty search in the search bar. If needed, use the filter on the left side to narrow down your search.

LinkedIn Learning

LinkedIn Learning is one of our primary training content providers that is integrated with WSCU. LinkedIn Learning provides online courses from industry experts with topics that range from business, creative, and technology. In order to take LinkedIn Learning courses on WSCU, you must have an active license which will be provided by Wright Service Corp.

Accept invitation

After you have been added to WSCU, you will receive a LinkedIn Learning invitation from Wright Service Corp. Please accept this invitation as soon as possible as the link will expire after two days. If your invitation expires, please reach out to LMSHelpDesk@wrightservicecorp.com to get another invitation. Select Get Started Now to accept the invitation.

Profile options

After you have accepted the invite, you have the option to connect you LinkedIn Learning account with your existing LinkedIn account or create a separate Learning account. If you happen to link your LinkedIn Learning profile with your personal account and would like to switch your work account, please contact LMSHelpDesk@wrightservicecorp.com.

Not able to access LinkedIn Learning from WSCU?

If you are not able to access or launch LinkedIn Learning courses from WSCU, please contact LMSHelpDesk@wrightservicecorp.com for assistance. Below is an image of an error if you are not in the LinkedIn Learning active directory group (this is used for Single Sign On).

LinkedIn Learning course not marking as complete

If you have watched a course or a custom learning path from WSCU, and it is not marking as complete you will need to reach out to LMSHelpDesk@wrightservicecorp.com for assistance. Please keep in mind to watch LinkedIn Learning courses from the window that is opened from WSCU. If you happen to link away to watch the course from linkedin.com/learning (not opened from WSCU), the course will not mark as complete on your transcript.

Transcript

Your transcript is where all of your assigned and elective training will be stored. To access your transcript, hover over the Learning tab in the blue navigation bar, and select View Your Transcript.

From your transcript, you can view Active, Completed, and Archived training by toggling the Active dropdown menu.

Course Not Launching

A common issue for a course that does not launch is due to pop-ups being disabled.

Enable pop-ups on Chrome

Select the menu button with three dots at the top right and then select Settings.

Then select the Advanced button at the bottom of the settings page.

Under Privacy and security, select Site Settings.

Next, select Pop-ups and redirects.

In the Allow section, add a new url from the Add button to the right. In the field add this url to allow pop-ups: https://wrightservicecorp.csod.com.

For more information on pop-ups click here.

Change Password

If you would like to change you password, navigate to the gear icon at the top right hand corner of the page and select My Account.

On the right side of the page, click on the Options dropdown button and then select Change Password.

Language Preferences

If you would like to change you language, navigate to the gear icon at the top right hand corner of the page and select My Account.

Select your preferred language from the Display Language tab.

After you select Save, you will be required to log out and log back into the portal to refresh the language setting.

Managers

Reports

Viewing reports on WSCU is a great way to see where your employees stand with their training status. Reporting 2.0 allows you to build custom reports based on an array of options and allows sharing reports with other users. You must be a manager to view reports.

Access reports

From the blue navigation bar, hover over Reports, then select Reporting 2.0

From the Reporting 2.0 page, you can see Quick Filters, My Reports, and Shared With Me on the left sidebar. Under Quick Filters, you can see System Templates, which are pre-built reports for you to view. To view the report, click on the tile or if you would like to export the report, click on the menu button with three dots, and select the format you would like to export.

Build report

My Reports is where you can view and build custom reports. To create a new report, select the Create Report button on the right side of the page.

In the report builder, you will need to provide a name for the report along with data that you would like to see in the report. To insert data in the report, select items from the left sidebar. You can also search for data to input such as Training Title, User First Name, User Last Name, Transcript Status, etc. After you select all of the data you woud like to see in the report, click Save.

Assign a Course for Managers

Step 1

From the Learner Home page, conduct a search for the course you would like to assign. For example, search “outlook” in the search bar to show results related to Microsoft Outlook.

Step 2

After you have searched and found the course you would like to assign, select the menu button at the bottom of the learning object. Then, select Assign from the menu items. If you cannot find a course or topic you would like to assign, reach out to the LMS Helpdesk at LMSHelpDesk@wrightservicecorp.com.

Step 3

After you select assign, you will be redirected to a page with assignment options. From here, you can see the course title, description, and length. You have the option to set a due date, add a comment, and select the users you would like to assign the course to.

Step 4

Select a due date or keep empty for no due date.

Step 5

Select who you would like to take the course. Once you have filled out all sections, select Submit to assign the course.
